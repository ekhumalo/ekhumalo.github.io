Bio

In 2013, I was chosen to be one of 32 intelligent, low income students to participate in the USAP program,founded by Rebecca Ziegler Mano in 1999. It paid SATs, application fees and gave me access to a pool of resources that have made me successful. Unfortunately, such opportunities in Zimbabwe are limited. My drive is to create more opportunities like USAP to give talented youths a chance to educate themselves and better their lives and communities, particularly in education, sports, arts and culture. Ethos

  • Dunamis Africa

    Founder and CEO

    Dunamis Africa motivates and empowers the Zimbabwean youth to strive for a better generation through the realization and utilization of talents in education, sports, arts and culture for the development of Africa.

  • ZimCode

    Project Partner

    The appreciation of technology through programming can unlock myriad opportunities. Zim Code aims to transform its participants into skilled, knowledgable and aware individuals that are able to use I.T entrepreneurship to better their lives and their communities, especially financially. The aim of the project is to groom young Zimbabweans into ambitious and technology-aware entrepreneurs who can create sustainable and viable employment for themselves and their embattled communities.

  • Excel Africa

    Co-Founder

    Excel Africa is an organization empowered by a myriad number of influential local and international students. Its primary purpose is to create an effective learning measure for both students and teachers.

  • Lead Us Today

    Co-Founder, Ant Drive Project- Mpopoma High School

    To inspire, mobilize and empower young people to work together beyond socioeconomic barriers and lead community development efforts.LUT focuses on how young people can build their entrepreneurial and leadership skills. Through a year-long program, it helps them identify local challenges, which they can solve together with their communities, using local resources. That way, they build experience and skills in innovating solutions to shared problems. Once they graduate, it does not matter whether the students are part of the 20% that pass or 80% that fail — they all have the very important skills to see and create opportunities that capture and create value for themselves and their communities. LUT is therefore, complementing an education system that, for decades, has failed millions of young Zimbabweans, and Africans.

Yelp Maps

Eric Khumalo with Victor Korir
CS61A

Goal

Creating a visualization of restaurant ratings using machine learning and the Yelp academic dataset. In this visualization, Berkeley is segmented into regions, where each region is shaded by the predicted rating of the closest restaurant (yellow is 5 stars, blue is 1 star) using a is a Voronoi diagram.
