For a moment

Laments of a broken heart

Nov 28, 2011 by Eric Khummalo 
                A dove, was I

                Spotless,

                Speckless,
                
                Admirable
                
                And straightforward.
                
                Arrogant to nature
                
                A social misfit.



                Then, 

                Entangled in this struggle

                With tricky nature

                In its unmeandering scheme

                Of getting its victim slain,

                A sweet voice, it is

                Piercing the arrogancy in my pump

                Reversing the reaction

                Befriending my enmity

                Look at what I am now

                A wailing piano,

                Solo-speaking
 
                          
                
                Stolen is the voice

                Voice so sweet

                Irreversible reaction

                Eyes filled to brim

                With pain evoked fluids

                My breakdown pump,

                Squeling with agony

                At this missing jigsaw puzzle

                The autistic mind of mine,

                Struck with obscurity,

                Turns to soliloquy

                What am I now,

                An outcast

                Discarded.



                But that I know

                Dawn comes with the sun

                To melt this 

                Mist of confusion

                The voice again shall come

                As sweet as the delicate song

                Of a honeybird

                Summoning me to pleasure

                And there will I stay 

                With the delicate voice

                Quenching my unquenchable thirst
